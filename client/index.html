📋 Trade Plan — Trading With An Edge Principles

"The simple, organized, consistent trader makes money. The overcomplicated, unorganized, inconsistent trader generally quits out of frustration." — Jared Wesley

1. Every Trade Has 3 Things
Every trade MUST have an Entry, Stop Loss, and Target. Without any one of these, there is NO trade. Know exactly how much you are risking before taking a position.
2. Approach Every Trade The Same
Consistency is the edge. Apply the same process, same patterns, same management every single day. Let statistical probability work in your favor over time.
3. Multiple Concepts Converging
The best trades have multiple factors converging in one area: price pattern, volume confirmation, moving average support/resistance, and pivot levels. More concepts = higher odds.
4. The 3 Chart Keys
Always ask: How did the bar form? Where did the bar form on the chart? How did it get to where it is? Individually they matter, but collectively they are powerful.
5. Only 3 Tools: Price, Volume, MAs
Keep it simple. Candlestick price action shows what is happening. Volume shows commitment. Moving averages (21 EMA, 200 SMA) confirm direction and dynamic S/R.
6. Fear & Greed Drive Markets
Markets are human emotion. When one side takes control for too long, expect a reversal. Track Buyers/Sellers in Control. Stay objective, don't let ego write checks.
7. Volume Tells The Story
3 volume types: Igniting (starts/continues a move), Ending (exhausts a move), Resting (allows continuation after pause). Price tells what, volume tells how.
8. Trade With The Trend
Uptrend = Higher Pivot Highs & Higher Pivot Lows. Downtrend = Lower Pivot Highs & Lower Pivot Lows. Smooth trends are easier to profit from than sideways ranges.
